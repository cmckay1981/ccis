# Human Factors for Offshore Drilling Operations Masterclass (HFO-MC)

## Course Overview

An advanced course applying Human Factors principles specifically to offshore drilling and marine operations. Built from IADC North Sea Chapter guidance and UK regulatory requirements, covering HF integration in MODU design, control room environments, barrier management, offshore work organization, and safety case compliance.

## Modules

### HFO-MC-101: HF Integration & Design (4 lessons)
Systematic HF integration in offshore projects and MODU design, including HFIP development, user-centered design, equipment layout, and verification.

### HFO-MC-102: Control Environments & Interfaces (4 lessons)
Drilling and marine control room design, human-machine interfaces, and alarm management for offshore operations.

### HFO-MC-103: Risk Assessment & Barrier Management (4 lessons)
Human Reliability Analysis, Performance Influencing Factors, bowtie analysis, and Major Accident Hazard scenarios.

### HFO-MC-104: Offshore Work Organization (4 lessons)
Offshore rotations, 12-hour shift patterns, fatigue risk management, and staffing levels for MODU operations.

### HFO-MC-105: Operational Integrity (5 lessons)
Procedures, permit-to-work systems, safety-critical communications, competence management, and supervision.

### HFO-MC-106: Regulatory Compliance & Safety Cases (5 lessons)
UK Safety Case Regulations 2015, HF requirements in safety case submissions, organizational change management, and continuous improvement.

## Learning Objectives

By completing this course, learners will be able to:

1. Apply HF integration principles throughout MODU design and modification projects
2. Evaluate control room design against ISO 11064 and EEMUA standards
3. Conduct Human Reliability Analysis for safety-critical drilling tasks
4. Identify and optimize Performance Influencing Factors
5. Apply bowtie analysis to understand human contributions to MAH control
6. Implement Fatigue Risk Management Plans for offshore operations
7. Assess staffing adequacy using recognized methodologies (CRIOP)
8. Design and implement effective alarm management systems
9. Develop procedural integrity linked to MAH scenarios
10. Prepare HF content for UK safety case submissions
11. Conduct accident investigations identifying latent organizational factors
12. Manage organizational change with HF impact assessment
